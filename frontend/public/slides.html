The Problem

Aadhaar VC gives us selective disclosure.
But businesses need more than yes/no claims.

UIDAI's Aadhaar VC is a major step forward — users control what to share, and isAdult avoids revealing DOB. But real business decisions aren't that simple.

  • No custom thresholds — A lending platform needs "age 25-55 AND income above 5 lakhs." There's no pre-computed claim for every possible business condition.
  • No duplicate detection — Detecting duplicate loan applications across platforms requires a shared identifier. SD-JWT produces no linkable, non-reversible token.
  • No cross-source proofs — Business rules combine Aadhaar + ITR + credit score. No single authority builds cross-credential verification.
The Solution

Three Tiers of Zero-Knowledge Proofs

A ZK proof engine on the user's device. Credentials in, boolean out. Private data never leaves.

Tier 1

Customizable Proofs

Single field, custom threshold. Unlike UIDAI's fixed isAdult, the threshold is configurable.

"Age ≥ 25" for home loan
"Age 30-45" for insurance
Tier 2

Composite Proofs

Multiple Aadhaar VC fields in a single proof. One source, multiple conditions.

"Age ≥ 25 AND resident of Maharashtra"
"Valid Aadhaar AND age ≤ 25 AND from UP"
Tier 3

Multi-Source Proofs

Aadhaar + ITR + Credit Bureau in one proof. No single authority can build this.

"Age 25-55 AND income 5L-50L
AND credit score ≥ 700"

Post-Quantum Security

Why ZK-STARKs?

UIDAI's Aadhaar VC uses RS256 (RSA) — a quantum-vulnerable signing scheme.
Xylem's proof layer is designed to survive quantum computing.

Property UIDAI SD-JWT (RS256) Xylem (ZK-STARKs)
Quantum-resistant No (RSA broken by Shor's) Yes (hash-based)
Trusted setup N/A Not needed (transparent)
Security basis RSA factoring SHA-256 / BLAKE3 hashes
When quantum arrives Signatures can be forged Proofs remain valid

ZKP security comes from mathematics, not secrecy. The circuit runs client-side — algorithm is public by design. Anyone can see how the proof is generated. No one can forge a proof without private inputs.
